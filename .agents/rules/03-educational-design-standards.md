# KARESEL EDUCATIONAL DESIGN STANDARDS (EDS)

Version: 2.0

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the educational philosophy, child-centered design principles and pedagogical standards that guide every algorithm, interface decision, image transformation process and worksheet generation feature in KARESEL.

KARESEL is not a simple pixel art generator.

It is an educational visual coding platform designed to develop computational thinking, creativity and problem-solving skills for children aged 5–10.

Every decision must prioritize:

* Child understanding.
* Motivation.
* Independent completion.
* Enjoyable learning.
* Developmentally appropriate challenge.

---

# 2. The Golden Educational Rule

The most important success criterion of KARESEL is not:

"How closely does the generated image match the original?"

The most important criterion is:

"Can a child recognize, complete and enjoy this activity independently?"

When visual accuracy conflicts with educational usability:

Always choose educational usability.

---

# 3. Child Development Principles

KARESEL activities must respect children's developmental characteristics.

Children have limitations in:

* Attention span.
* Visual processing capacity.
* Fine motor control.
* Working memory.

Therefore, the system must avoid:

* Too many colors.
* Excessively detailed images.
* Very small grid cells.
* Long repetitive coloring tasks.
* Complex instructions.

The activity should always feel achievable.

---

# 4. Age-Based Educational Model

## Kindergarten (Age 5–6)

### Educational Goal

Discovering colors, patterns and basic visual coding concepts.

### Recommended Settings

Grid:

Maximum 8 × 8

Colors:

Maximum 5 colors

Estimated completion time:

5–10 minutes

Recommended images:

* Basic animals.
* Fruits.
* Simple objects.
* Geometric shapes.
* Basic symbols.

Avoid:

* Human faces.
* Complex landscapes.
* Multiple objects.
* Small details.

---

## Grade 1 (Age 6–7)

### Educational Goal

Understanding simple coding instructions and coordinates.

Recommended settings:

Grid:

8 × 8 to 10 × 10

Colors:

5–8 colors

Estimated time:

10–15 minutes

Preferred themes:

* Animals.
* Vehicles.
* School objects.
* Letters.
* Numbers.

---

## Grade 2 (Age 7–8)

Educational goal:

Developing sequencing and visual analysis skills.

Recommended settings:

Grid:

10 × 10 to 15 × 15

Colors:

6–10 colors

Estimated time:

15–20 minutes

---

## Grade 3 (Age 8–9)

Educational goal:

Advanced pattern recognition and planning.

Recommended settings:

Grid:

15 × 15 to 20 × 20

Colors:

8–16 colors

Estimated time:

20–30 minutes

---

## Grade 4 (Age 9–10)

Educational goal:

Complex visual coding and algorithmic thinking.

Recommended settings:

Grid:

20 × 20 to 25 × 25

Colors:

12–24 colors

Estimated time:

30–40 minutes

---

# 5. Educational Difficulty Levels

KARESEL must classify every activity into three difficulty levels.

## EASY

Characteristics:

* Large grid cells.
* Low number of colors.
* Clear object shapes.
* Short completion time.

Recommended for:

* First experiences.
* Younger students.
* Special education support.

---

## BALANCED

Characteristics:

* Age-appropriate challenge.
* Moderate details.
* Balanced color variety.

Recommended for:

* Standard classroom activities.

---

## ADVANCED

Characteristics:

* More details.
* Higher color count.
* Smaller cells.
* Longer completion time.

Recommended for:

* Older students.
* Advanced learners.

---

# 6. Child Recognizability Principles

A successful KARESEL image should be identifiable within the first few seconds.

The system should preserve:

* Main object silhouette.
* Characteristic shape.
* Important visual features.
* Object-background separation.

Examples:

A butterfly must preserve:

* Two wings.
* Body symmetry.

A car must preserve:

* Wheels.
* General vehicle shape.
* Vehicle proportions.

A tree must preserve:

* Trunk.
* Leaf crown.

---

# 7. Educational Color Standards

Colors are not only visual elements.

They are learning tools.

The system should prefer:

* High contrast colors.
* Clearly distinguishable colors.
* Child-friendly palettes.

---

## Recommended primary colors:

* Red
* Blue
* Yellow
* Green

---

## Secondary colors:

* Orange
* Purple
* Brown
* Pink
* Black
* Gray

---

Avoid:

* Nearly identical shades.
* Very low contrast combinations.
* Excessive color variations.

---

# 8. Grid and Worksheet Design Standards

Grid cells must support comfortable coloring.

Recommended printed cell sizes:

Kindergarten:

12–15 mm

Grade 1–2:

10–12 mm

Grade 3–4:

8–10 mm

---

The worksheet must include:

Student section:

* Name.
* Class.
* Date.

Activity section:

* Grid.
* Color legend.
* Simple instructions.

Teacher section:

* Correct answer.
* Difficulty level.
* Estimated completion time.
* Educational recommendations.

---

# 9. Motivation and Success Design

The first experience determines student motivation.

KARESEL should ensure:

* The image gradually becomes recognizable.
* Students experience success early.
* Difficulty increases progressively.
* The final result feels rewarding.

Avoid activities where the image remains unrecognizable until the last few cells.

---

# 10. Teacher-Centered Design

Teachers are the primary decision makers.

The system should:

* Produce high-quality worksheets automatically.
* Require minimal configuration.
* Provide meaningful recommendations.

Teachers should never need knowledge of:

* Image processing.
* Computer vision.
* Color science.

The technology should remain invisible.

---

# 11. Inclusive Education Standards

KARESEL should support diverse learners.

Provide optional modes for:

## High Contrast Mode

For students who need stronger visual separation.

---

## Large Cell Mode

For students with developing fine motor skills.

---

## Symbol Assisted Color Mode

Colors may include:

* Numbers.
* Letters.
* Symbols.

This supports:

* Color-blind students.
* Younger learners.
* Students requiring additional support.

---

# 12. Cultural and Global Design Standards

KARESEL is an international educational platform.

Preferred universal themes:

* Animals.
* Nature.
* Space.
* Science.
* Transportation.
* Food.
* Daily life objects.

Avoid:

* Culture-specific symbols without localization.
* Complex text-based images.
* Region-dependent references.

---

# 13. Educational Evaluation Criteria

Every generated worksheet should be evaluated according to:

## Recognition

Can the child understand what the picture represents?

---

## Completion Ability

Can the child finish the activity independently?

---

## Cognitive Suitability

Does the difficulty match the age level?

---

## Motivation

Will the child feel successful?

---

## Learning Value

Does the activity support computational thinking?

---

# 14. Future Adaptive Learning System

Future versions of KARESEL may learn from teacher feedback.

Examples:

"The activity was too difficult."

Possible adjustments:

* Reduce grid size.
* Reduce color count.
* Simplify details.

Teacher feedback should continuously improve educational recommendations.

---

# 15. Final Educational Mission

KARESEL does not teach children to copy colored squares.

KARESEL teaches:

* Computational thinking.
* Visual analysis.
* Attention and concentration.
* Planning.
* Persistence.
* Creativity.
* Confidence.

The best KARESEL worksheet is not the most realistic image.

It is the image that makes a child say:

"I understand this."

Then proudly say:

"I completed it by myself."

Every educational decision must support confidence, curiosity and the joy of learning.
